One way of looking at a makefile is as a tool for executing a series of steps to build the contents of a directory. A better way is to look at is as a way to programmatically anyalyze the desired artifacts that a build process produces, and then the relevant substeps for constructing each artifact, and then executing those steps. (This inversion, switching from "what steps do I execute?" to "what artifacts do I want to produce?" is drawn from Artifact-Based Build Systems, a section in Software Engineering at Google (an excellent read).)
